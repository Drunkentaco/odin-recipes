Beef Stew

Everybody needs a beef stew recipe, this one will take around 3-4 hours, but once done, you'll want to make it again!

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds beef chucks (cut into 1 inch)
  • 4 teaspoons salt and pepper
  • 2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Sunflower oil (canola is fine)
  • 1-2 onions (cut into 1-inch)
  • 7 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons sugar
  • 4 large carrots (cut into 1 inch)
  • 4 celery (cut into 1 inch)
  • 1 pound potatoes (cut into 1 inch)
  • Cornstarch (if needed)
  • Fresh chopped parsley

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325Â°F and set a rack in the lower middle position.
  2. Pat the beef dry and mix it with flour, salt, and pepper
  3. In a heavy soup pot, heat around 1 tablespoon sunflower oil over medium-high heat until hot and shimmering.
  4. Brown the meat on each side (DON'T OVERCROWD THE MEAT) for about 5 minutes
  5. Add one more tablespoon of oil for each batch and repeat step 4 until no meat left
  6. Transfer the meat to a large plate or bowl and set aside.
  7. Add the onions, garlic and balsamic vinegar and cook for 5 minutes (scrap brown bits with wooden spoon)
  8. Add the tomato paste and cook for a minute more.
  9. Add the beef with its juices back to the pot.
  10. Add the wine, beef broth, water, bay leaf, thyme, and sugar and bring to a boil (scrap brown bits with wooden spoon)
  11. Cover the pot with a lid, transfer to the preheated oven, and braise for 2 hours.
  12. Remove the pot from the oven and add the carrots, celery, and potatoes.
  13. Cover and place back in the oven for about an hour more, or until the vegetables are cooked, the broth is thickened, and the meat is tender.
  14. If broth not thickened enough, make slurry (2 tablespoons cornstarch +2 tablespoons of water) and boil for 3 minutes until desired thickness.
  15. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  16. Garnish with fresh parsley, if desired. Bon appetit!
